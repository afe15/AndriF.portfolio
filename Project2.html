Pressfit model

Step 1:

First thing to do was to download the software: Fusion360, from their website.

It took some time to think of a project i wanted to make, even after looking for inspiration on these 3 websites: Thingiverse,Thangs and Makerworld. In the end i just decided to design my own Headphone stand and just starting doodling concepts, with these 3 being the first ideas:

Not finding one idea better then any other, i decided to combine some of their aspects for the final consept, with initial rough estimates on the dimensions.

Step 2:

I had no experience using the Fusion360 software so in the beginning I had to watch alot of educational videos both from the teatcher and what I found searching on Youtube. The videos that helped the most are: For getting to know Fusion360, Setting up parameters and Making holes for finger-joints, though there were many other bits and peaces of videos I took a glance at that helped.

I also decided that i wanted to use pressfit/finger-joint to fasten the arms to the bottom and some sort of a clamp to fasten the top to the arms.

Step 4:

For testing the fastenings I exported a test drawing as a DXF file with the help of this video, and cut it out with the Kerf just set to 1 mm. After cutting i measured my test structure in order to get an accurate scale for Kerf like this:

And by noting the difference between the the edges of the large hole and the length of the cubes clumped together, I could deduce that the right setting for Kerf should be 0.421 mm. With the correct setting for Kerf i could start cutting out test versions of the clamp and finger-joint fastenings, and the test results showed predictably that my crude clamp fastening did not fit, though my finger-joints did. Heres what the succesive finger-joints test looked like:

Knowing this I redisgned my clamps to use for the final structure. It took 3 different clamp designs to finally find one that would work for my project, with the third one being the one that was chosen.

Step 4:

With all the tests completed, it was time to cut out the whole thing. I messured the the thickness to be: 6.44 mm instead of 6 mm. Then i just had to change the "efni" parameter in fusion and all my other parameters and mesurement scaled to fit. The new mesurements became:

Now it was just a matter of exporting the DXF again, now for the whole structure, and dividing the different parts in Inkspace for individual cutting. Pressing the 4 parts together was a bit more difficult then I expected, but in the end it fit. This is the result:

Overall im happy with the final look, altough the top sags just a bit, but not enough to be a problem.
